Media Elements

Media elements in HTML refer to the elements used for embedding multimedia content like audio and video into web pages. These elements allow web developers to provide rich and engaging multimedia experiences to their website visitors. The primary media elements in HTML are <audio> and <video>. Let's explore them in detail:

<audio> Element:

  • The <audio> element is used for embedding audio content, such as music, sound effects, or podcasts, directly into a web page.
  • Attributes:
  • src:Specifies the source URL of the audio file.
  • controls:Displays audio playback controls (play, pause, volume, etc.) to the user.
  • autoplay:Automatically starts playing the audio when the page loads (use with caution).
  • loop: Enables continuous looping of the audio.
  • Example:
<audio src="music.mp3" controls>
Your browser does not support the audio element.
</audio>

<video> Element

  • The <video> element is used for embedding video content, such as movies, video clips, or video tutorials, into a web page. structured menus.
  • Attributes:
  • src: Specifies the source URL of the video file.
  • controls:Displays video playback controls (play, pause, volume, etc.) to the user.
  • autoplay:Automatically starts playing the audio when the page loads (use with caution).
  • loop: Enables continuous looping of the audio.
  • width and height: Define the dimensions of the video player.
  • Example:
< video src="video.mp4" controls width="640" height="360">
Your browser does not support the video element.
</video>

File Types:

  • HTML5 supports various audio and video formats, including MP3, Ogg, WebM for audio, and MP4, WebM, Ogg for video. It's a good practice to provide multiple formats to ensure compatibility across different browsers. looks.
